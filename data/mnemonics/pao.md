# PAO 00–99

Person–Action–Object for general number memorisation. Separate system from the birth/death scenes in `mnemonic_implementations.md` — no birth/death grammar applies here.

## How it's built

- **Person** — from the character peg list in `major_system_pegs_0-100.md`. Phonetics (two-initials, Major System) decide who is *eligible* for a number.
- **Action** — derived from the person, **not** from phonetics. Freddie Mercury doesn't need an F·M verb; his action is belting into a half-mic stand. This is standard PAO practice and it's what makes the space spannable: every person has a characteristic act, so there are no gaps, and distinct people rarely do the same thing.
- **Object** — from the object peg list in `major_system_pegs_0-100.md`, phonetic as usual.

## The selection rule

**Phonetics decides who is eligible. Action-distinctiveness decides who is primary.**

This matters because the character pool is now footballer-heavy, and a primary chosen on phonetics alone would give a dozen numbers whose action is "kicks a ball". A footballer only takes primary where the signature act is unmistakable and unique — Bobby Moore lifting the cup, Vinnie Jones grabbing Gazza, Schmeichel's diving save. Otherwise they drop to alternate.

Actions are **not derivable**. If you forget one you can't reconstruct it phonetically — you recall it through the person, which is the point of PAO.

## Open decisions

- **Dance is the crowded family** — moonwalk (36), hips (43), gold hotpants (73), dad-dancing (92), Brent dance (46). Five. Under pressure, 43 and 92 are the two to re-cut.
- **Nobody is doing "kicks a ball"**, which is the point. Every footballer primary has a *specific* act. If one starts blurring into another, the alternates in `major_system_pegs_0-100.md` are the bench.
- **Pairs can't act.** "Sid & Nancy" (02) and "Tom & Jerry" (16) were fine as static character pegs but a pair can't perform one clean action. Sid & Nancy is replaced by **Sam Neill** (S,N = 02) — staring up at a dinosaur. Tom & Jerry survives because "setting a mousetrap" is a single act with a single agent. Sid Vicious himself is S,V = **08**, and sits as an alternate there behind Sam Fox.
- **Alternates live in `major_system_pegs_0-100.md`.** Every person demoted from primary is still on the bench there — if a primary's action goes stale, swap the person, not the number.
